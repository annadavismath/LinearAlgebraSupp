\begin{exercise}
Suppose three planes given by the equations below intersect at a single point.
$$\begin{matrix}
      a_1x& +&a_2y&+&a_3z&=&0\\
      b_1x & +&b_2y&+&b_3z&= &0\\
      c_1x&+&c_2y&+&c_3z&=&0
    \end{matrix}$$

What can be said about the vectors $\begin{bmatrix}a_1\\b_1\\c_1\end{bmatrix}$, $\begin{bmatrix}a_2\\b_2\\c_2\end{bmatrix}$ and $\begin{bmatrix}a_3\\b_3\\c_3\end{bmatrix}$?   

Select all that apply.

\begin{selectAll}
 \choice{Nothing can be deduced from the information given.}
 \choice[correct]{The three vectors are linearly independent.}
 \choice{The three vectors are linearly dependent.}
 \choice{The three vectors are pairwise orthogonal.}
 \choice{The first vector can be written as a linear combination of the other two.}
 \choice[correct]{The equation $\alpha\begin{bmatrix}a_1\\b_1\\c_1\end{bmatrix}+\beta\begin{bmatrix}a_2\\b_2\\c_2\end{bmatrix}+\omega\begin{bmatrix}a_3\\b_3\\c_3\end{bmatrix}=\vec{0}$ has only the trivial solution.}
 \end{selectAll}
 
\end{exercise}
